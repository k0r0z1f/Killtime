Dwarven Population and Society Overview

The dwarves are a long-lived and cautious species, with a steady population growth and a stable, conservative economy. Their society revolves around craftsmanship, mining, and architecture, and is shaped by their unique lifespan and reproductive cycle.

Key Characteristics

  • Species: Dwarves, average lifespan of 370 years (maximum 500 years).
  • Reproduction: Monogamous, with reproduction starting at age 20 and ending around 400 years of age.
  • Storms and Disasters: Frequent storms and infrastructural damage lead to a focus on resilient city planning.
  • Economic Growth: Steady and conservative, with careful resource management.

Key Considerations

  • Lifespan: The dwarves' long lifespan results in slow, steady population growth.
  • Reproduction: Limited window for reproduction, leading to controlled population increases.
  • Storms and Infrastructure: The dwarves' cities are built with resilient architecture to withstand frequent storms.
