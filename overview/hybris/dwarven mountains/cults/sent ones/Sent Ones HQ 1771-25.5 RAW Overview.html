Population

The Sent Ones' HQ houses a scattered group of former cultists, many of whom still cling to their beliefs despite the organization’s fall from power. The population includes Lucas, Mina, Thomas, Dira, Tolgar, and a mix of cultists—some desperate, others faithful, all marked by the decay of their once-formidable faith. These people live in fractured unity, struggling to maintain their purpose and relevance as the prophecy unfolds around them.

Appearance

The Sent Ones' HQ is a shadow of its former self, with crumbling stone walls and remnants of the grandeur it once possessed. The hallways are dimly lit, casting eerie shadows on faded tapestries and symbols. The air feels heavy with the weight of a forgotten legacy, and the space feels both haunted and urgent as the cult clings to its once powerful role in Hybris.

Energy & Atmosphere

The atmosphere within the HQ is thick with tension and quiet reverence. The remaining cultists, although fractured, still cling to their faith, creating an air of suspicion. Despite their dwindling power, there’s an underlying sense of destiny at play—prophecies and secrets linger in the walls, driving the cult's dwindling resolve. Desperation runs deep as the cult clings to relevance, unsure of how the unfolding events will affect them.

Architecture

The headquarters is constructed of ancient, sturdy stone—practical and austere. It was once designed for ceremonial purposes, with large chambers and stone benches arranged to accommodate rituals. The space has grown cold and functional over time, its grandeur stripped away, but the original layout remains intact, serving as a reminder of the cult’s former might.

Symbolism & Carvings

The walls are adorned with cracked and faded symbols from the Sent Ones' lore—glyphs and ancient carvings that once depicted major events and prophecies. Many of these symbols are now difficult to decipher, with years of decay and neglect having obscured their full meanings. However, their power still resonates within the HQ, marking the space as a place of prophecy, both revered and feared.

Defenses

The defenses within the HQ are minimal, relying more on old rituals and the presence of the remaining cultists for protection. There is no clear, physical defense or fortress around the location, leaving it vulnerable. The real danger lies in the powerful secrets and prophecies hidden within, whose influence can shape the course of events and potentially alter the minds of those who come into contact with them.

Legends & Lore

The Sent Ones' lore is deeply tied to ancient prophecies, the powerful upheaval of the world, and the involvement of great figures such as Lucas, Mina, and Thomas. The cult was once a mighty force, guiding the world towards an era of change, but now it stands at a precipice. Their lore speaks of an impending apocalypse, of great powers awakening, and of a legacy that cannot be undone. The HQ remains a place of both reverence and fear as these ancient powers begin to stir again.

Hidden Dangers

Despite the outward appearance of an abandoned fortress, the Sent Ones' HQ is rife with hidden dangers. The collapse of Goldar’s reign has left the cult fractured, and internal dissenters could be hiding in plain sight. The artifacts the party is investigating are powerful, with unknown consequences for those who come into contact with them. The cult's leadership is divided, and the true dangers may lie not in the walls, but in the shifting alliances and secrets still buried within.

Link to the Party

The party has entered the Sent Ones' HQ in search of answers about the prophecy and the ancient artifacts tied to their fate. They are closely watched by the cult’s leadership, though they have not yet come under direct threat. The party's role here is one of discovery and negotiation, seeking the cult's resources while trying to understand the cryptic messages within the artifacts. They are drawn deeper into the mystery, guided by the cryptic words of the Council and their pursuit of knowledge.

Surrounding Terrain

The Sent Ones' HQ is surrounded by rugged, isolated terrain. Distant mountain ranges stretch across the horizon, creating a natural barrier that isolates the location from the rest of Hybris. The area feels remote and cut off, contributing to the somber and oppressive mood of the HQ, as if the world outside has long since forgotten this once-powerful place.

Weather

The weather surrounding the Sent Ones' HQ is harsh and unforgiving. Cold storms frequently lash the area, cutting off all but the most determined travelers. The bleak, inhospitable conditions add to the feeling of isolation and contribute to the HQ's somber atmosphere. It’s a place where hope seems to wither beneath the weight of both the environment and the unrelenting passage of time.

Recent Developments

The Sent Ones' HQ is in a state of uncertainty. With the prophecy coming to fruition, the leadership of the cult is fractured, and new external threats are emerging. The Empire is growing increasingly interested in the artifacts and the cult’s connection to ancient prophecies, threatening the fragile peace within the HQ. The most recent development is a message from the elves of the Citadel, Haliriel, requesting a meeting with the party—an indication that the stakes are rising and alliances are shifting rapidly.
