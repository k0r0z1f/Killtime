Population

The Nythari tribe is a small, close-knit community of around 280 members. They live harmoniously with the jungle, surviving off the land and maintaining a peaceful, sustainable existence. The villagers are resilient and deeply connected to nature, their way of life shaped by centuries of tradition and survival.

Appearance

The Nythari people are known for their elegant and striking appearance. With their amber eyes and long brown hair, they embody the natural beauty of the jungle. Their clothing is crafted from materials found in nature, blending seamlessly with the environment. Their presence exudes resilience, strength, and a deep connection to the land.

Energy & Atmosphere

The energy in the village is calm and grounded, with a deep, ancient connection to nature. While the atmosphere is generally peaceful, there is a tension in the air due to the looming threat of outside forces that seek to exploit the land and its people. Despite this, the Nythari maintain their focus on survival and spiritual harmony with their surroundings.

Architecture

The architecture of the Nythari village is simple and nature-integrated. The huts are constructed from wood, stone, and plant materials that blend with the surrounding jungle. Treehouses and sacred sites are interwoven with the environment, reflecting the tribe's deep reverence for nature. The structures are designed to be both functional and harmonious with the land.

Symbolism & Carvings

The Nythari tribe's symbols are deeply tied to their relationship with nature. Emblems of the mountain hawk and other animals are carved into trees, rocks, and structures, representing their spiritual connection to the land. These carvings also tell stories of the tribe's survival and their ongoing resistance against external threats, including the Empire.

Defenses

The Nythari tribe's defenses are not built with fortifications, but rather rely on stealth and their deep connection to the jungle. The tribe uses natural obstacles, traps, and camouflage to protect their village. Nythari warriors are skilled in using the environment to their advantage, making them formidable opponents in their own land.

Legends & Lore

One of the most important legends in Nythari culture is the tale of The Watcher, an ancient protector of the land. This figure is said to embody the spirit of the jungle and the strength of the Nythari ancestors. The tribe also tells stories of their survival against the Empire’s oppression, keeping alive the memory of their resilience and defiance.

Hidden Dangers

The jungle that protects the Nythari people can also be a source of danger. It is filled with wild animals, treacherous terrain, and hidden threats. The jungle’s thick vegetation can easily conceal predators, and the ancient ruins scattered across the landscape may hide traps or dangerous artifacts.

Link to the Party

Candice's connection to her people is vital to her sense of responsibility toward the Nythari tribe. Her departure from the village to fulfill her mission has left her with mixed feelings of duty and guilt. Her knowledge of the land and its defenses is crucial to the party as they navigate the dense jungle and face potential threats from outside forces.

Surrounding Terrain

The Nythari village is surrounded by dense jungle, steep slopes, and high cliffs. The land is lush with vegetation, rivers, and valleys, making it both a beautiful and challenging place to navigate. Hidden ruins can occasionally be found, but the terrain is largely unexplored and untouched by outsiders.

Weather

The weather is typically warm and humid, with occasional storms that roll through the jungle. Higher elevations tend to be cooler, providing some respite from the heat. The unpredictable rain showers add to the mystery and difficulty of living in such a dense and ancient environment.

Recent Developments

Recent developments in the region include talk of the Kingston explosion and potential involvement with the Children of Hybris. This has led to Candice’s departure from her village, leaving her people behind to fulfill her mission. Her journey may have larger implications for both the Nythari tribe and the unfolding prophecy.
