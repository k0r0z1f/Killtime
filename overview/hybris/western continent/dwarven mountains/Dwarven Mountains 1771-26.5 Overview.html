Population

The Dwarven Mountains are home to a few outsiders and a predominantly dwarven population. There are 12 cities and numerous small settlements scattered throughout the mountains, with the dwarves leading the way in mining and craftsmanship.

Appearance

The Dwarven Mountains are known for their towering, jagged peaks and steep cliffs. The rocky terrain is snow-covered, with ancient stone structures, mines, and fortresses scattered throughout. Deep caves and tunnels cut through the mountains, leading to hidden chambers and dwarven settlements.

Energy & Atmosphere

The energy of the Dwarven Mountains is isolated and cold. The atmosphere is tense, with a deep-rooted sense of power, stoicism, and discipline. The region remains sovereign, and while the dwarves are fiercely proud, there is an underlying unease as the world beyond the mountains presses in.

Architecture

The architecture of the Dwarven Mountains is a blend of functional robustness and ancient design. Stone fortresses and mining tunnels dot the landscape, while underground and skyward structures show off dwarven craftsmanship. The ruins and caves carved into the mountains tell stories of a long, proud history.

Symbolism & Carvings

The mountains are rich with ancient runes and carvings. These include clan symbols, protective runes, and powerful engravings on the mountainsides and around key gates and tunnels. The carvings signify strength, power, and protection, often tied to their history and mythology.

Defenses

The Dwarven Mountains are heavily fortified with natural defenses. High cliffs and mountain passes make access difficult, while underground fortresses and watchtowers along key passes provide additional protection. The dwarven military is formidable, and cave systems provide natural defense routes that are hard to penetrate.

Legends & Lore

Legendary stories of dwarven heroes and ancient artifacts abound in the Dwarven Mountains. There are tales of powerful conflicts with dereks, and the ruins scattered around the mountains are rich in dwarven history and craftsmanship, with many ancient secrets still hidden within the stone.

Hidden Dangers

The terrain of the Dwarven Mountains is treacherous, with unstable cave systems, dangerous wildlife, and territorial beasts. Political intrigue among the dwarven clans adds a layer of danger for those who wander too far into the wrong areas. Cave-ins and deadly wildlife are not uncommon.

Link to the Party

The Dwarven Mountains play a crucial role for the party, particularly with the dwarven council. However, outsiders often face resistance, and the party's movements will be restricted by the growing tension between the dwarves and outside forces, including the World Police and the elves.

Surrounding Terrain

The surrounding terrain is rocky and mountainous, with high altitudes and steep cliffs. Snowfields dominate the higher elevations, while forests appear at lower altitudes. Dangerous wildlife, from territorial beasts to massive mountain predators, roam the land.

Weather

The weather in the Dwarven Mountains is harsh, characterized by long, cold winters and frequent snowstorms. High winds and occasional thunderstorms make travel difficult, especially during the colder months. Snow and precipitation are a constant threat, with the weather remaining cold and unforgiving.

Recent Developments

Recent tensions have surfaced between the Dwarven Mountains and neighboring regions, including the World Police. The dwarves remain largely autonomous, but their growing mistrust of outsiders, particularly elves, has led to heightened tensions. This has created a volatile environment for the party as they navigate the region.
