Population

The Council Chamber is home to the Dwarven council, consisting of about 10–15 members, including the influential elders Aria and Gorla. The chamber also hosts those involved in the trial, such as the defendants and various attendees.

Appearance

The chamber is an awe-inspiring space, with carvings that depict legendary judgments and epic battles. The dome above is adorned with glowing veins of gold and silver, giving the room a sacred and powerful aura.

Energy & Atmosphere

The atmosphere in the chamber is tense and serious, especially as deliberations become more critical. The mood shifts dramatically when Thomas reveals crucial information, creating a growing sense of alarm and urgency.

Architecture

The architecture of the Council Chamber is a marvel of dwarven engineering, with towering columns, intricate stonework, and a grand circular table where decisions are made. The glowing veins in the dome represent the balance of power in the chamber.

Symbolism & Carvings

The carvings in the chamber tell stories of dwarven law, including past judgments that have shaped the political and judicial landscape. These symbols serve as reminders of the chamber’s central role in preserving dwarven history and justice.

Defenses

The chamber is well-guarded with physical barriers and security measures, ensuring that proceedings remain free from external interference. The importance of the council’s decisions makes its protection vital.

Legends & Lore

The chamber is tied to ancient prophecies and past judgments that have defined dwarven society. Elder Aria represents a line of revered leaders who have helped guide the dwarves through turbulent times.

Hidden Dangers

The hidden dangers within the council chamber are primarily political in nature. Shifting alliances, betrayals, and outside interference—especially from the World Police—threaten the stability of the chamber and the council’s decisions.

Link to the Party

The party’s fate hinges on the outcome of their trial in the chamber. Their future is tied to the political and judicial decisions made here, and they must navigate the complex dynamics at play to secure their freedom.

Surrounding Terrain

The Council Chamber is located deep within Brum'korath, a dwarven stronghold carved into the heart of the mountain. It is surrounded by other chambers and structures, which add to the chamber’s sense of isolation and security.

Weather

The weather outside Brum'korath is cold and oppressive, mirroring the grim mood inside the chamber. The harsh environment reflects the weight of the trial and the uncertainties facing the dwarves.

Recent Developments

Recently, the chamber has hosted major events, including the trial of Goldar from the Sent Ones. The party’s conditional freedom hinges on the outcome of their trial, and tensions surrounding the fallout from the Disciple's destruction continue to rise.
