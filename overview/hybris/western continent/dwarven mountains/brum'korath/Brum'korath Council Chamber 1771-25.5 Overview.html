Location Name

The Council Chamber is a grand and ancient space, located at the heart of the dwarven stronghold of Brum'korath.

Population

The Council Chamber is home to a small but powerful group of Elders, numbering around 10 to 15 members, including the prominent Elders Aria and Gorla.

Appearance

The chamber is awe-inspiring, with intricate carvings depicting legendary judgments, lawgivers, and monumental battles. The ceiling dome shimmers with faint veins of gold and silver, creating a sacred atmosphere.

Energy & Atmosphere

The atmosphere is tense and serious, with a palpable sense of foreboding. The energy within the chamber shifts from cautious deliberation to alarm after Thomas' revelation.

Architecture

The architecture is a marvel of dwarven engineering, with towering columns, intricate stonework, and grand designs. The central circular table and glowing veins in the dome represent the balance of power and history.

Symbolism & Carvings

The walls are filled with carvings depicting legendary figures, battles, and ancient judgments, emphasizing the importance of dwarven law and the weight of governance.

Defenses

While no specific details are provided, it is implied that the chamber is well-guarded, potentially protected by enchantments or physical barriers to prevent any interference.

Legends & Lore

The chamber holds a rich history, as shown by the carvings and symbols, referencing past judgments, monumental events, and lawgivers. Elder Aria's position suggests a deep connection to a long line of wise and respected leaders.

Hidden Dangers

The hidden dangers lie in the political maneuvering within the council. The trial could bring betrayals, shifting alliances, or even outside interference, such as from the World Police, complicating matters further.

Link to the Party

The party (Lucas, Mina, and Thomas) are central to the trial and their fate will be determined within these walls. Their role in the destruction caused by the Disciple is key to the proceedings.

Surrounding Terrain

The chamber is carved deep within the mountain, surrounded by other chambers in Brum’korath, a stronghold of dwarven civilization. The city is nestled among deep mines and possible underground passages.

Weather

While the atmosphere inside the chamber is heavy and tense, the weather outside is cold and oppressive, reflecting the grim mood of the city following the destruction of the Disciple.

Recent Developments

The destruction of the Disciple, political instability, and the Empire's involvement all contribute to the tension within the council chamber. The outcome of the party's trial will have significant consequences for the future.
