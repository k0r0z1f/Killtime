Design

Compact, rugged, utilitarian design for practicality and stealth. Built for efficiency and durability in challenging environments.

Materials

Constructed with reinforced alloys and composite armor, providing durability while keeping the ship's weight manageable for stealth operations.

Size

The Starlight Voyager is medium-sized, comparable to a small frigate, offering a balanced capacity for both crew and operational needs.

Sails/Membranes

The ship does not have sails or membranes, relying on thrusters for atmospheric control.

Levitation Mechanism

Uses antigrav boosters and stabilizers for smooth and efficient levitation in both atmosphere and space.

Propulsion

The ship is powered by ion engines and magnetic thrusters, offering a balance between energy efficiency and propulsion power.

Interior Design

The interior is minimalistic and utilitarian, with few luxuries, focusing on practicality for long-term missions and stealth operations.

Cockpit

The cockpit is centrally located for the pilot, offering manual flight control assisted by basic AI for navigation and mission management.

Energy Source

The ship is powered by a core fusion reactor, with backup solar cells for extended missions.

Navigation

Standard star maps and manual calculation tools assist in navigation, ensuring precise course plotting during deep space travels.

Speed

The ship is moderately fast in atmosphere, though slower in deep space, where efficiency is prioritized over speed.

Range

Long-range capabilities allow the ship to travel for several months in deep space before requiring resupply.

Stealth

The ship is designed for low-profile operations, with stealth features like a camouflaged hull and cloaking capability to avoid detection.

Altitude Limitations

In atmosphere, the ship is limited to mid-range altitudes, while it has no limitations in space.

Special Features

Features include a camouflaged hull, cloaking capability for stealth operations, and modular components that can be adjusted for various mission types.

Passenger Capacity

The ship has a capacity of 8-10, accommodating the crew along with a small complement of guests.

Exterior Appearance

The ship's exterior is a dark, matte gray, with a weathered surface and a faint glow emanating from the reactor vents.

Flight Control

Flight control is manual, with basic AI assistance to ensure smooth navigation and mission control.
