Role/Background

The Chief of the Tribal Guards leads his people in their defense against outside threats. His tribe was devastated by the Empire, and he now struggles to protect what remains of his people and their sacred land in the jungle.

Personality Traits

The Chief is deeply scarred by the loss of his tribe. He is mistrustful and bitter, especially toward outsiders and the Empire. His harshness and suspicion are tempered by a quiet willingness to negotiate when it benefits his people. He speaks in a guttural, almost unintelligible tone but demonstrates tactical acumen and resilience.

Abilities/Magic

The Chief excels in survival tactics, particularly in the jungle. He is a skilled leader, guiding his people through guerilla warfare, and is proficient in various forms of combat, utilizing his knowledge of the terrain to his advantage. His leadership is critical in the fight against imperial forces.

Alliances/Clans/Factions

He leads a tribal faction that is firmly opposed to the Empire, fighting to protect the remnants of their ancient land. His tribe remains defiant despite the Empire’s overwhelming strength.

Appearance

The Chief is a tall, rugged figure, draped in a cloak adorned with intricate tribal symbols. His battle-worn cloak marks him as a leader and survivor of the Empire’s brutality. His face is marked with scars that tell of past conflicts, and his eyes reflect the pain of loss.

Character Arc/Goal

His primary goal is to protect his remaining tribe and the sacred forest, despite the heavy toll the Empire has taken on his people. His arc explores themes of grief, survival, and leadership in the face of overwhelming adversity.

Secret

The Chief carries the painful secret of his tribe’s destruction by the Empire. This trauma influences every decision he makes, and it shapes his distrust of others, especially those connected to the Empire.

Combat Training

The Chief is highly skilled in guerrilla warfare, particularly in the jungle environment. His expertise allows him to lead his forces effectively in battle and utilize the terrain to his advantage. His leadership in combat is as essential as his tactical skills.

Inventory

The Chief carries a knife that symbolizes the heritage of his tribe. It is not only a weapon but a token of leadership and the sacrifices his people have endured.
