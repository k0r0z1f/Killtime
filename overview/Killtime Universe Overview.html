Killtime - Comprehensive Overview

1. Hybris

Year 0: Uninhabited until approximately 3,000 deported individuals arrive in cages made of rare metal alloys. Leadership is established, but small clans form among the diverse population.

  • Dwarves: Migrated west to the mountains.
  • Elves: Headed south to the great forest.
  • Remaining Population: About 1,600 individuals stayed near the initial landing site (the cages).
  • Dereks: The native race of Hybris, who live in the mountainous regions and have magical control over the earth.

Year 1771: The year Lucas and Mina attend college.

Description: A newly colonized planet characterized by a harsh environment and undergoing ecological stabilization. The young ecosystems are in a state of flux, presenting challenges for inhabitants, including survival against the elements and resource scarcity.

Inhabitants:

  • Deported Population: 3,000 individuals from advanced technological civilizations, forming small clans with distinct identities and cultural practices.
  • Dereks: The native race of Hybris, known for their magical abilities and earth-based control.

Cultural Impact: The integration of magic as a form of 'science' affects social hierarchies, alliances, and conflicts among clans, leading to power struggles and differing beliefs about the use of magic.

2. Vardis

Description: A planet characterized by rugged landscapes and a mix of primitive and developed areas, serving as a crucial point in the larger conflict. The terrain includes rocky mountains, dense forests, and expansive plains, reflecting the scars of war and imperial exploitation.

Climate: Diverse climate with regions experiencing harsh winters, dry spells, and occasional storms, influencing the inhabitants' lifestyle and survival strategies.

Inhabitants:

  • Primitive Peoples: Various indigenous tribes with distinct cultures and survival strategies, many steeped in magic.
  • Imperial Settlers: Pockets of imperial settlers with advanced technology, leading to tensions with indigenous populations.

Societal Structure:

  • Tribal Factions: Each tribe has its own hierarchy, often based on elder councils or chiefdoms.
  • Resistance Groups: Alliances formed to resist imperial control, utilizing guerrilla tactics and traditional knowledge.
  • Imperial Military Presence: Strict control maintained by imperial forces, enforcing curfews and deploying advanced weaponry.

Magic and Belief Systems: Magic linked to nature, with different tribes harnessing various types of magic reflecting their environments and cultural beliefs, sometimes leading to ritualistic practices that involve intense emotional experiences.

3. Apaphis

Overview: A war-torn planet marked by ongoing conflicts and imperial exploitation, featuring diverse ecosystems including desolate wastelands and ruined urban areas. The scars of battle are evident, creating a tense atmosphere where survival is paramount.

Inhabitants:

  • Primitive Peoples: Tribal societies connected to the land, skilled in survival, often using their magical abilities to maintain a balance with their environment.
  • Imperial Settlers: Remnants of settlers engaging in resource extraction, causing friction with native tribes.

Societal Structure:

  • Tribal Factions: Hierarchies based on elder councils or chiefdoms, leading to conflicts over resources and territory.
  • Resistance Movements: Alliances formed by tribes to resist imperial control, characterized by acts of bravery and sacrifice.
  • Imperial Forces: Militarized regime using advanced technology and propaganda to maintain order, often employing brutal methods.

Magic and Belief Systems: Magic intertwined with the tribes' beliefs, viewed as a gift from the land, influencing their worldview. The understanding of magic is deeply spiritual, often involving intimate connections with the forces of nature.

4. Asteroid Base

Description: A concealed command center located within an asteroid, orbiting near Apaphis. This base serves as a hub for resistance planning and strategy.

Functionality: Equipped for planning missions and analyzing enemy positions, prioritizing efficiency over comfort. The atmosphere is filled with the tension of looming danger and urgency, as lives depend on their success.

Atmosphere: Gritty and filled with the constant hum of machinery, reflecting a sense of urgency and purpose. Shadows lurk in corners, adding to the sense of isolation.

5. Imperial City

Description: A fortified urban area heavily guarded with strict military control. Its imposing architecture symbolizes the strength and oppression of the imperial regime.

Location: Located on Vardis, this is a critical site for the resistance’s infiltration plans.

Significance: The city's oppressive atmosphere serves as a stark contrast to the freedom sought by the rebels.

6. Magic System

Nature: Viewed scientifically; governed by principles similar to physics, rare and elemental in its application.

Elements: Different magic types resonate at unique frequencies, influencing elemental affinities and enhancing characters’ abilities. This resonance determines how individuals connect with their powers, often leading to intense, transformative experiences.

Technology: Magical technology hints at the integration of magic with engineering, affecting social dynamics and hierarchies, often showcasing the power disparity between the elite and the commoners.

Integration: Magic corresponds to physical properties (e.g., temperature, pressure) and can be harnessed, manipulated, or converted, blurring the lines between natural and artificial.

Resonance: Each type of magic resonates at a specific frequency, influencing characters’ capabilities and elemental affinities, creating unique pathways for personal growth and connection with others.

7. Themes and Motifs

  • Duality of Power: Explores the interplay between physical prowess and magical abilities, and their societal implications.
  • Sacrifice and Loyalty: Characters face tough choices involving loyalty, survival, and the cost of progress.
  • Environmental Struggles: The delicate balance between magic and nature forms the core struggle against oppressive forces.
